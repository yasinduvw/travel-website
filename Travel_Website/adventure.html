Hot Air Ballooning

Adventure takes on new meaning as the multi-colored hot-air balloon drifts silently above the forest.

A couple of peacocks take flight directly below the balloon's basket, startled by the noise of a sudden burst of gas keeping the balloon up in the air, their outrageously long opalescent blue and emerald tail feathers streaming behind them like a psychedelic jet stream. Several deer dart nervously across the clearing below, but the small herd of elephants continues to feed, unfazed by the aerial intruder.

Balloons that travel with the wind provide one of the most surreal and peaceful flight experiences. After the initial adrenaline rush of takeoff, one could relax and take in the beauty of the colorful earth below.

Rock Climbing

This four-hour tour takes you to one of Sri Lanka's few natural rock climbing areas that has been outfitted with climbing safety bolts. Three bolted and cleaned rock faces can be scaled, with 15 different routes of varying difficulty. One solid rock with high friction provides excellent starting points for climbing.

The activity is led by experienced climbers who use high-quality, international-standard equipment. While climbing up and belaying down, you are always attached to a safety harness. An expert climbing guide and belayer, climbing and safety equipment, on-site instruction, and transportation from the meeting point to the site and back are all included in the price. Prices vary according to the number of people in the party.

Scuba Diving

The ocean around Sri Lanka whispers of regal maritime visits, bandits and pirates, and explorers and spice merchants' journeys. These stories come to life as you dive into the warm waters below. The Indian Ocean's crystal clear waters, with visibility of 20 meters (65 feet), are ideal for diving. Deep reefs and reef walls provide enthralling explorations for scuba divers, with wrecks dating back to the 18th century dotting the seas. On-shore coral reefs are ideal for snorkeling for the less adventurous.

The abundance of marine life found throughout the island is only surpassed at Hikkaduwa on the southern coast, where the reef is thought to contain more fish species than the Great Barrier Reef.

Whale Watching

When you embark on a whale/ dolphin watching excursion off the Southern, Eastern, or Western coasts of Sri Lanka, the ever smiling, mischievous dolphins will put on a show, while some of the oldest and largest sea creatures, the humpback and blue whales, will nonchalantly glide past you. Dondra Point (accessible from Galle, Hikkaduwa, and Mirissa) and Trincomalee are ideal for whale watching, while the sea off Kalpitiya is teeming with dolphins.
